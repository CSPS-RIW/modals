LGBTQ2+ Questions

1. I have never had to come out regarding my sexual orientation or gender identity.
2. My gender identity matches the sex I was assigned at birth.
3. I can use public restrooms without fear of verbal abuse, or physical intimidation.
4. Strangers don’t assume they can ask me what my genitals look like and how I have sex.
5. If I end up in the emergency room, I do not have to worry that my gender will keep me from receiving appropriate treatment, or that all of my medical issues will be seen as a result of my gender.
6. I frequently see my gender as an option on forms.
7. Expressing affection in most social situations does not illicit hostile or violent reactions from others.
8. I can move to another country and not have to worry about whether my partner and I will have equal access to job opportunities.
9. Growing up, the movies and shows I watched depicted romance and relationships of people of my sexual orientation.
10. I don’t have to deal with old photographs that did not reflect who I truly was.
11. I identify as a man.
