Privilege Activity

In this activity, the course guides will be do a privilege walk. They will either step forward, or backwards, in response to the statements presented about their lived experiences.

Click through the statements to see how they impact their levels of privilege. If you’d like a reminder, click here to review the descriptions of each guide.

LGBTQ2+ Questions

1. I have never had to come out regarding my sexual orientation or gender identity.
2. I have consistently seen members of my racial, ethnic, or religious, group portrayed on television in a degrading or stereotypical way.
3. Individuals I engage with tend to pay too much attention to my impairments instead of the barriers I encounter and tend to make decisions on my behalf based on their perception of my disability / disabilities.
4. I can access employer mental health support from a caregiver who understands my experience.
5. I am free to express affection in social situations without experiencing looks of shock, disgust, or hostility.
6. I can go home from most work meetings to feeling somewhat included, rather than isolated, out-of-place, outnumbered, unheard, held at a distance or feared.
7. I have a disability (visible or invisible) or functional impairment.
8. I believe my mental health status has resulted in my exclusion in professional and / or social settings.

Guide introductions

Hi! I’m Megan. I’m a daughter, a woman, and a public servant. I’m also First Nation Cree from treaty Six territory and I’ve been working with the federal public service for five years. In my off time, I love to listen to music and attend live music events. I’m also a lesbian and my fiancé and I are in the process of planning our wedding. We are both very excited!

Hi, I’m Robert. I am a third-generation Black Canadian man and I’ve been working with the Federal government for a few years. I’ve been enjoying my career for the most part, however, I have experienced some barriers related to my dyslexia and more recently, my anxiety. I’ve also simultaneously experienced racism, which has made it more difficult for me. I am an avid birdwatcher and am a yearly contributor to the backyard bird count.

Hello, I’m Morena! I’m a mixed-race cisgender woman of European and Central American descent. I grew up and lived in Honduras, and I moved to Canada after completing my Master's degree. I have been working as an administrative clerk for the last two years and am looking for opportunities to progress in my career. I was one of the fastest sprinters in my country growing up and I still compete.

Hello, my name is Alex. I am a white man of European decent who was recently diagnosed with a mental illness. I am an analyst in the federal public service and I love working on briefing notes and providing strategic advice – I enjoy telling good stories. It has been a little more difficult for me in the past few months as I had to take some time off due to my mental illness. I enjoy writing poetry and I have recited my work at several local events.
